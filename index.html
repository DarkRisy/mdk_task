Springtrap

William Afton

Springtrap is an early model of a Bonnie suit . He is a heavily damaged rabbit animatronic of a dirty olive drab color. A great portion of his right ear is missing with wires sticking out of the stump left behind. Springtrap's entire body is torn and tattered with gaping holes exposing caging and wires throughout, thus making Springtrap appear much more macabre-looking than any of the other animatronics from previous installments. While both hands and feet are attached and generally intact, his legs are entirely stripped from the mid-shin down, fully exposing endoskeleton metal and wiring. From his right hand, half of the middle finger is missing. Both of Springtrap's eyes are properly set inside his head and appear to be a pale gray.

Spring Bonnie

William Afton

Springy Bonnie has never been shown in full, appearing only in mini-games. Based on them, the Spring Boney is a whole version of the Springtrap. Its body is golden in color, its belly is colored lighter than the main body. He also had a decorative button on his body and a purple bow tie. There are five fingers on his palms, which is associated with the ability to wear it as a suit. He had white lenses, and the color of his eyes remains unknown. There is also a lever on the body that allows you to switch the animatronic mode of the costume. His endoskeleton has the ability to move apart to fit a person there.

Scraptrap

William Afton

This animatronic is a damaged Springtrap that survived the fire at the Phasebear Horror attraction caused by a security guard. However, when Afton finally takes on a new form, his longtime friend and colleague Henry Emily, who founded the franchise with William Afton, unleashes a certain call that three robots eager to kill must respond to: The Destroyed Baby, Scraptrap and the Fused Freddy. Despite the fact that each of the possessed robots decides to follow the deceptive call, William Afton turned out to be smarter than the others and immediately suspected a trick, but still decided to respond out of interest.
